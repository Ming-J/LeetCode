The purpose of Event Management is to detect events, make sense of them and determine the appropriate control action.

Key dimensions to be monitored are the availability, reliability, maintainability and serviceability of technology infrastructure.

How long:

  • Incidents are handled on a priority basis. 

How:

  • All incident tickets will be raised and managed in ServiceNow 
  • Tickets are managed according to Impact and Urgency, which together calculate the ticket Priority: 
    • Impact: Single User / Department / Office / UK Firmwide
    • Urgency: Low / Normal / Urgent / Critical
  • Status updates will be communicated via ServiceNow tickets
  • Users are able to add notes and chase incidents by opening the ticket and entering comments. Ticket links are automatically provided when tickets are initially setup

Who:

  • Incidents will be allocated to the apropriate team member based on location and availability
  • Escalations can be made to Hiten Gohil or Pritesh Patel, who will include relevant parties as required
  • Incident investigation may involve liaison with parties outside of DMS including ITS 

Where:

  • Onsite presence in four sites: Crawley - Park House, Crawley - Global House, London - Moorgate, London - Chancery Lane. 
  • For onsite support in the main London campus buildings (New Street, Hill House etc), support is provided by one of the four sites listed above
  • All incidents to be submitted to Service Delivery team mailbox, this will generate a ServiceNow Incident ticket. Where possible, the following details should be included:
    • Project name e.g. "Rameses" 
    • Name of hardware/software application impacted e.g. "CMS Pro"
    • Incident symptoms e.g. "Redress calculator is not loading"
    • Examples e.g. Case ID 1234, 4567, 7890 or URL link that is causing issues 
    • Username(s) e.g. pritepatel
    • Time of incident occurence e.g. 9am 13 Dec 2018
    • Impact: nature and number of users impacted e.g. 5 users unable to load redress calculator
    • Location of impacted user(s) e.g. Crawley Park House, 2nd floor
  • Note: best practice for submitting incidents is to use project name and description in the email subject (e.g. "Rameses - Monitor not working" as opposed to "Help") as this title is used in the ServiceNow queue managed by the team 
  • Additional information will be requested from the user by the Service Delivery team depending on the particular incident 
  • A ServiceNow Incident ticket will be raised by the DMS IT Service Delivery team if they notice any failures

The purpose of Incident Management is to restore normal service operation to technology hardware or software as quickly as possible after an issue and minimise the adverse impact on engagements.

The DMS IT Support team will Facilitate/Raise/Manage Incident requests in Service Now, this has the following advantages:

  • Ensures standardised methods and procedures are used for efficient and prompt response, analysis, documentation, ongoing management and reporting
  • Increases visibility and communication of incidents to engagements
